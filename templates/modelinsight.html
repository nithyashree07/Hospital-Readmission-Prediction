Model Insights

"Understand the inner workings and advantages of our predictive model."

  Overview of Our Predictive Model

Our state-of-the-art predictive model is designed to anticipate patient readmissions with high accuracy, enabling healthcare providers to take proactive measures. By analyzing comprehensive patient data, our model provides actionable insights that help reduce readmission rates and improve overall patient care.

 How the Model Works?

  • Data Collection and Processing : We collect a wide range of patient data, including demographics, medical history, lab results, and treatment details. This data is then cleaned, normalized, and prepared for analysis to ensure high-quality input for the model.
  • Feature Selection and Engineering : The model identifies key factors, such as age, lab results, and previous hospital visits, that significantly impact readmission risk. These features are meticulously engineered to improve model performance and accuracy.
  • Model Training and Optimization : Our model is trained using a diverse set of patient data to learn patterns and make accurate predictions. It is continually optimized to enhance its predictive power and adapt to new data.

 Model Performance Metrics

Our model achieves high accuracy and precision, ensuring reliable predictions. Performance is evaluated using:

Accuracy: 78%

"Overall Correctness of Predictions"

Accuracy is a measure of how often the model's predictions are correct. It calculates the percentage of all cases — both patients predicted to be readmitted and those predicted not to be — where the prediction matches the actual outcome. High accuracy means the model is generally reliable across all its predictions.

Precision: 71%

"Reducing False Alarms"

Precision focuses on the quality of the predictions when the model predicts a patient will be readmitted. It tells us how many of those predicted readmissions were correct. High precision means that when the model predicts a readmission, it is often right, minimizing incorrect predictions of readmission.

Recall: 84%

"Capturing All True Cases"

Recall measures the model's ability to correctly identify all patients who will be readmitted. It focuses on minimizing cases where the model fails to predict an actual readmission. High recall is essential in healthcare settings where missing a potential readmission could lead to inadequate patient care or oversight.
